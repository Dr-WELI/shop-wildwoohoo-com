This is the opening set of the Kangaroo Time line. One mob of eastern grey kangaroos, the same animals across four booklets, watched through summer, autumn, winter and spring. Each booklet takes the same mob and looks at it from a different angle, so a child sees how one group of animals changes as the year changes around it.

The booklets are field guides, not storybooks. They are short, fact-led, and built to be carried outside. Each one positions the reader as a researcher with a notebook: look closely, write down what you see, then go and find it in the real world. The science is real and named. Heat regulation comes from Dawson, mob structure from Jarman, joey development from Tyndale-Biscoe, play behaviour from Burghardt. Every fact in every booklet traces to a published source printed in the back.

Each booklet is bilingual, English and Portuguese on facing pages, and each carries a code for the free companion. The slipcase holds all four.

What this is not. It is not a picture book and there are no talking animals. The kangaroos do not narrate. This is a set of small field guides that treat a young reader as someone capable of real observation.
